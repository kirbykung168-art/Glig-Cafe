№ 02 · The Acronym

G·L·I·G — good luck I guess.

GLIG is an acronym for "good luck I guess" — the shrug-emoji philosophy of opening a small bakery cafe in Bangkok and letting whatever happens, happen. The logo is a four-leaf clover, the Japanese symbol for good luck. The interior is a glasshouse, because if you're going to bet on light and weather, you might as well bet on it generously.

"Nestled deep in the quiet landed-residential enclave within Sukhumvit is Japanese glasshouse-inspired Glig. This beautiful space with its lush adjoining garden is best visited on a sunny late morning." — heartpatrick · travel/spaces · Jan 2023

The room is mostly cream, pale wood and clear glass, with the green of the garden pressing against the windows on three sides. The bakery does choux cream puffs in honey-yuzu, vanilla custard and hazelnut — all at the same ฿95 because the math is simple. The signature drink is a milk slushie you choose-your-own-adventure: pour an espresso shot over the top (฿140) or a swirl of matcha cream (฿220).

The Instagram handle is @gliggligglig. That's three "glig"s because the single-glig handle was taken and the founders decided three of anything is better than one — which is also a four-leaf-clover kind of reasoning.
