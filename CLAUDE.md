# GLIG CAFE — Sukhumvit 49, Bangkok

A minimalist Seoul-glasshouse single-page tribute to GLIG (Good Luck I Guess) — a Japanese-inspired bakery cafe with a four-leaf-clover mark, on Sukhumvit 49/13.

## Brand brief

- **Name:** GLIG CAFE. The acronym = "**Good Luck I Guess**". Logo: a four-leaf clover (Japanese good-luck symbol).
- **Concept:** Japanese-inspired glasshouse bakery cafe. Sleek glasshouse interior, natural light, lush garden, photo-worthy corners.
- **Location:** 219 Sukhumvit 49/13, Khlong Tan Nuea, Watthana, Bangkok 10110
- **Hours:** Daily 08:00–20:00 (per Daniel Food Diary 2022, mindtrip 2025)
- **IG:** @gliggligglig · **FB:** /gliggligglig

- **Display:** Inter Tight 300/400 (clean minimalist Korean register)

- **Thai:** Noto Serif Thai

## Animations — brand-coherent (8)

1. **Sun-wash sweep** — scroll-tied light wash moves across the page as you descend
2. **Four-leaf-clover gentle spin** on logo hover (ties to "Good Luck I Guess")
3. **Soft leaf-sway parallax** on garden photos
4. **Choux puff reveal** — soft cream blob expand on menu hover
5. **Lucky clover Easter egg** — type "glig" and a four-leaf-clover scatter fires
6. **Marquee** with clover separators
7. **Hand-drawn nav underline** in clover green
8. **Hero crossfade** between two signature interior shots

## Structural moves (5)

1. Asymmetric airy hero (lots of negative space, glasshouse light)
2. **Huge clover-photo wordmark** with garden photo bleeding through "GLIG" via background-clip:text
3. **Bento menu** — choux puffs 2x, slushie callout, glasshouse photos
4. **Hand-arranged polaroid garden gallery**
5. **Manifesto in brand voice** around "Good Luck I Guess"
